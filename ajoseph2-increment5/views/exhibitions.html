Current Exhibition

The World of Jean-Michel Basquiat

Running through June 30, 2026

This exhibition brings together a curated selection of works spanning throughout Basquiat's career.

Featured Works

Highlights include studies of his iconic crown motif, anatomical drawings that blur the line between body and diagram, and his layered text-based compositions that are like visual poems.

Interactive stations allow visitors to explore the recurring symbols Basquiat used throughout his career.

About the Artist

Jean-Michel Basquiat (1960–1988) came from the streets of New York City to become one of the most celebrated artists of the 20th century. Self-taught and original, his work challenged the art world's conventions.

He draws on influences from jazz, hip-hop, African history, and street culture. He became the first Black artist to achieve international status in the contemporary art world, all before his death at age 27.
